    Lucid dreaming and dream control are not the same thing.

    When you first become lucid you might say in your head I want to fly. That doesn't mean you will fly. You need to control your brain by giving it a reason TO fly. Your brain is still conditioned to not being able to fly so you have to overcome that barrier first.

    Most of us don't have a particularly great imagination in the day. We can imagine single elements of something at a single time. When we want to go larger we usually have to have some kind of medium to hold our ideas - such as a piece of paper. If you want to do something in lucid dreaming you need to already have a plan.     I have come across many resources that tell you that you shouldn't do X in a dream because you might confuse it with reality... and that would be REALLLLLLY BAD!

    While some dreams might have a quality to them, you are never going to confuse them with reality for two quite simple reasons:

    1. You chose what the dream would be... it is not like you didn't know you were doing it.
    2. A lucid dream is never so real as to be confused with reality... while you can do cute things in a lucid dream, it never quite passes for reality.
    The only way you could actually confuse a dream for reality in the light of day, is if you had some underlying medical condition. I have a sneaking suspicion that if that were the case, you wouldn't have any control over your lucid dreams anyway.
